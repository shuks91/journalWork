Employability Skills

For the duration of the boot camp we have an employability tutor available to us. Every week, we cover topics that will increase our chances of securing a job in the industry. In our first session we discussed job titles and salaries - it prompted me to think about how I would like a companies ethos and values to align with my own. It's essential for me to work in a place that has a positive culture and sparks my passion and interest.

We also talked about how to make the most of the upcoming weeks in boot camp while still maintaining balance in our daily lives. Software boot camps can be intense with a heavy workload and high expectations. That's why i'm working on being kind to myself emotionally and mentally during this time. Personally, what helps is to remind myself of my motivations for signing up, stay committed, do my best, and try to maintain good habits throughout the course.

Collectively, we delved into the components of a compelling CV, how to capture a recruiter's attention, the significance of a recent and relevant CV and incorporating noteworthy personal accomplishments. Discovering that a CV typically garners a mere 8 seconds of scrutiny was both enlightening and startling. This emphasises the importance of making those initial moments count. Before the next session I am going to rework my CV with a focus on what I want prospective recruiters to notice first. A key takeaway I noted was to explore the available templates on Canva for inspiration.
We also touched on tailoring cover letters to a specific role and organisation. Perusing the company's website and leveraging the job description makes for a cover letter that will increase the odds of being shortlisted.

As someone who has been out of the job market for a few years taking an in-depth look into how to approach job hunting has increased my confidence.

Things i'll be thinking about and reviewing after each week:

• Career Options and Job Hunting

• Confidence

• Goal Setting

• Future Focus

• Self-awareness
